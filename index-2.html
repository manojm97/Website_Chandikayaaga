About Sahasra Chandika Yaga

It is carried out with full fervor and virtue. Matha Chandika is the fiercest manifestation of Goddess Durga. She is considered as the mother of the Universe and an embodiment of feminie power and Energy. She is also believed to be the creator of the Holy Trinity of Lord Brahma, Lord Vishnu and Lord Shiva.

Sahasra Chandika Yaga is considered as the Supreme Form of Worship of Goddess Durga. Chandika Homa is done to appease Blessings of Goddess Durga. The name Chandi is the Other Name of Goddess Durga as mentioned in Devi Mahatyam. The homa in the presence of divine power will help us face all adversities in life and business and achieve success.

MAHA YAGA CONDUCTED BY

Late Shri Vishvesha Swamiji

1008 Pejwara Mutt Pontiff

Sri Vishvesha Tirtharu was the 32nd in the lineage of the Pejavara matha, starting from Sri Adhokshaja Tirtharu, who was one of the direct disciples of Sri Madhvacharya. He was the honorary president of Vishva Tulu Sammelana. He had established Poornaprajna Vidyapeetha in Bangalore which has completed over 63 years. Many scholars are trained here on Vedanta.

Shri Subhudendra Swamiji

1008 Mantralaya Mutt Pontiff

Shri Subhudendra Swamiji known to foster knowledge and knowledge seekers. He was fond of Vedic Literature and always encouraged his younger sin, Pandita Kesari and Mahamopadyaya Vidwan Raja S. Giri Acharya to learn the Shastras & to gain Vedic Wisdom. As indicated by his learned guru and father, Vidwan. Sri Raja S. GiriAcharya grew up to be a scholar of repute.

Shri Raghuvarendra Swamiji

1008 Bheemanakatte Mutt Pontiff

Sri Raghuvarendra Teertha Swamiji, Bheemanakatte Matha, Teerthalli, took his sanyaasa deeksha from his guru Sri Sri Raghumanya Theertha Swamiji on the auspicious occasion of Sri Sri 1008 Vadiraja Teertha Swamiji Aradhana Mahotsava in 2009. In his poorvashrama Sri Sripadaru did his Sudhamangala under the guidance of Sri Sugunendra Theertharu.

Shri Vishwaprasanna Swamiji

1008 Pejwara Mutt Pontiff

Sri Vishwaprasanna Theertha Swamiji is 33rd Swamiji in the lineage. Born in 1964 at Haleyangady-Pakshikere in Dakshina Kannada district to Krishna Bhat and Yamunamma, successfully running 1 disabled School in Neelavara, 1 Goshala named Neelavara Goshala with 2500 cattle in Neelavara and 1 Gurukulam named Sri Prahallad Gurukulam Teaching Spiritual and Vedic Education
